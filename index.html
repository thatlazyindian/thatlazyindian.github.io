about me

I am a data scientist and I am passionate about customer-led growth and analytics.

My history with data started at Course5i where I worked as a Research Analyst right after my undergraduate. Using randomized experiments and statistics, I modeled survey data to measure consumer perception. I am an engineer at heart and love behavioral economics. I used (then nascent) AI tools (like NLP, computer vision) to develop data-driven products for advertisement and research industries and co-ordinated with the business development team for both pre and post sales activities.

Inspired by the transformational power I saw data can have while simultaneously aware that I needed to skill-up, I applied and was accepted into Carlson School of Management, University of Minnesota’s Masters in Business Analytics program. This one year accelerated program is centered around four pillars of analytics i.e. descriptive, predictive, causal and prescriptive. During my time at Carlson, I not only learned crucial analytical skills but I also interned with three start-ups and attended multiple events (in one of them presented my work on Graph Theory). Some of my projects done at Carlson have been shared on my blog.

Post graduation, I joined Northwestern Mutual (NM), a Fortune 100 insurance firm. During this time, I provided data-driven thought partnership for NM’s strategic initiatives, with a focus on client experience.

In August 2023, I joined Cisco, where I am responsible for improving customer experience with Cisco’s products and services.
